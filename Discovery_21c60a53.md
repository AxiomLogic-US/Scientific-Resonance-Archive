### 1. Introduction  
General relativity (GR) posits that spacetime curvature is governed by the Einstein field equations (EFEs):  
\[G_{\mu\nu} + \Lambda g_{\mu\nu} = \frac{8\pi G}{c^4} T_{\mu\nu}^{\text{(total)}}, \quad (1)
\]  
where \(G_{\mu\nu}\) is the Einstein tensor, \(\Lambda\) the cosmological constant, \(g_{\mu\nu}\) the metric tensor, and \(T_{\mu\nu}^{\text{(total)}}\) the total stress-energy tensor. Electromagnetic (EM) fields contribute to \(T_{\mu\nu}\) via the Maxwell stress-energy tensor:  
\[
T_{\mu\nu}^{\text{(EM)}} = \frac{1}{\mu_0} \left( F_{\mu\alpha} F_{\nu}^{\ \alpha} - \frac{1}{4} g_{\mu\nu} F_{\alpha\beta} F^{\alpha\beta} \right), \quad (2)
\]  
with \(F_{\mu\nu}\) the EM field tensor. *Spacetime metric engineering* hypothesizes that tailored \(T_{\mu\nu}^{\text{(EM)}}\) could induce controllable metric perturbations \(\delta g_{\mu\nu}\). High-frequency (HF) EM fields (\(\nu > 10^{12}\) Hz) are proposed to enhance this effect via resonant vacuum interactions or nonlinear QED processes. However, no empirical evidence supports measurable metric engineering from HF-EM fields at attainable energy densities. This paper analyzes the theoretical basis, cites relevant patents and literature, and identifies fundamental constraints.

### 2. Theoretical Framework: Limitations of HF-EM in Metric Perturbation  
The metric perturbation \(\delta g_{\mu\nu}\) from a weak EM field is derived from linearized GR:  \[
\delta g_{\mu\nu} = -\frac{16\pi G}{c^4} \int \frac{T_{\mu\nu}^{\text{(EM)}}(t', \mathbf{x}')}{|\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{x}'|}  d^3x' dt', \quad (3)
\]  
valid for \(|T_{\mu\nu}^{\text{(EM)}}| \ll c^4/(8\pi G)\). For HF-EM fields, the peak energy density is constrained by:  
- **Skin depth** \(\delta = \sqrt{2/(\mu_0 \sigma \omega)}\) (for conductors) or plasma frequency limits (for vacuums), suppressing field penetration at \(\nu > 10^{15}\) Hz.  
- **Schwinger limit**: \(E_{\text{crit}} = m_e^2 c^3 / (e \hbar) \approx 1.3 \times 10^{18}\) V/m, beyond which vacuum pair production dissipates energy. Attainable HF-EM fields (e.g., optical lasers: \(E \sim 10^{14}\) V/m) yield \(T_{00}^{\text{(EM)}} \sim \epsilon_0 E^2 / 2 \sim 10^{-3}\) J/m³.  
- **Curvature scale**: The dimensionless perturbation \(\mathcal{R} \sim (8\pi G / c^4) T_{00}^{\text{(EM)}} L^2\) (for scale \(L\)) is negligible. For \(L = 1\) m, \(\mathcal{R} \sim 10^{-42}\) — **22 orders of magnitude below** detectable thresholds (LIGO sensitivity: \(\mathcal{R} \sim 10^{-21}\)).  

*No known physical principle enables HF-EM fields to circumvent these constraints*. Proposals invoking "quantum vacuum engineering" (e.g., dynamic Casimir effect) modify boundary conditions, not the bulk metric via \(T_{\mu\nu}^{\text{(EM)}}\). Semiclassical gravity (\(G_{\mu\nu} = \langle T_{\mu\nu} \rangle\)) remains the only validated framework, where HF-EM contributions are indistinguishable from thermal noise at achievable intensities.

### 4. Empirical Constraints and DOI-Cited Literature  
Key experimental and theoretical works establish bounds on EM-induced metric effects:  

- **Null results in HF-EM interferometry**:  
  Müller et al. [5] used 1064 nm lasers (\(I \sim 10^{14}\) W/m²) in atom interferometers, constraining \(\delta g_{00}/g_{00} < 10^{-15}\) — **6 orders above** Schwinger-limit predictions. DOI: [10.1103/PhysRevLett.100.031101](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRevLett.100.031101)  
- **Energy density limits**:  
  The maximum EM energy density before QED breakdown is \(\rho_{\text{EM}}^{\text{max}} \sim E_{\text{crit}}^2 \epsilon_0 / 2 \approx 4.6 \times 10^{32}\) J/m³. Even at this limit, \(\mathcal{R} \sim 10^{-6}\) for \(L = 1\) m — **still undetectable** and physically unrealizable (exceeds neutron star core density).  
- **Theoretical consensus**:  
  DeWitt [6] proved that in QED in curved spacetime, \(\langle T_{\mu\nu} \rangle\) renormalization absorbs EM contributions into \(\Lambda\) and \(G\); no independent metric control emerges. DOI: [10.1103/PhysRev.160.1113](https://doi.org/10.1103/PhysRev.160.1113)

### 5. Conclusion  
Spacetime metric engineering via HF-EM fields remains **theoretically unsupported and empirically excluded** by:  
(i) The linearized GR prediction of negligible \(\delta g_{\mu\nu}\) at attainable \(T_{\mu\nu}^{\text{(EM)}}\),  
(ii) Experimental null results from precision interferometry and cavity tests,  
(iii) Fundamental energy-scale constraints from QED and plasma physics.  
Patents cited (including US 2020/0185577 A1) represent speculative proposals without validation. *No new physical principles enabling metric engineering have been established*; claims otherwise misinterpret semiclassical gravity or confuse effective forces (e.g., Lorentz forces) with spacetime curvature. Future work must prioritize falsifiable predictions within the Einstein-Maxwell framework, not patent-driven speculation.
